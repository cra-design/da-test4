Learn about your taxes

Develop an understanding of how the Canadian tax system works

Learn about your taxes: national competition for middle and high school students

Students and teachers, explore the Learn about your taxes competition and your school could win a share of a $5,000 prize pot! This competition runs from September 2nd to October 15th on a gamified content engagement platform. The competition is for Canadian middle and high school students, and teachers. The competition is designed to boost students' tax literacy and confidence. Don't miss out - Check out the Learn about your taxes competition today! Note that participants will be required to create a free account to access the competition and that clicking on the link will navigate you away from Canada.ca to a third-party website.

Take this online course to learn about personal income taxes in Canada.

Modules

  1. Purpose of taxes

    An introduction to what taxes are, why they are collected, and how Canada's tax system works.

    • 4 lessons
    • 21 minutes total

  2. Starting to work

    What you need to know when you start working, why you need a social insurance number (SIN), and your responsibilities when reporting your income.

    • 2 lessons
    • 8 minutes total

    Working for an employer

    When to fill out a TD1 form, and what’s on your pay stub and T4 slip.

    • 3 lessons
    • 27 minutes total

    Working for yourself

    Understanding self-employment income and your responsibilities, how to manage your goods and services tax/harmonized sales tax (GST/HST) account, and how to report your income.

    • 3 lessons
    • 19 minutes total

  3. Preparing to do your taxes

    Find out what you’ll need to know before doing your taxes and the different ways to do them.

    • 3 lessons
    • 27 minutes total

  4. Completing a basic tax return

    An introduction to a basic income tax and benefit return. What you need to report, how to claim deductions and tax credits, and finding out whether you will get a refund or owe tax.

    • 7 lessons
    • 76 minutes total

  5. After sending us your tax return

    What you’ll need to know after you’ve done your taxes, including understanding your notice of assessment, paying off a balance owing, and interacting with the Canada Revenue Agency (CRA).

    • 4 lessons
    • 38 minutes total

  6. Accessing your benefits and credits

    Some of the benefits and credits the CRA administers, what you might be eligible for, and how to keep getting your benefit payments.

    • 2 lessons
    • 19 minutes total

  7. Saving for the future

    An introduction to various registered plans, what they are, and how to open them.

    • 1 lesson
    • 10 minutes total

  8. Using My Account

    The services available through this online portal, how to access them, and why you may want to register.

    • 2 lessons
    • 12 minutes total
